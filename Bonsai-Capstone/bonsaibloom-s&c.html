Broom Style

The Broom Style features a straight, upright trunk with branches forming a flat canopy. This style resembles a broom and is popular for deciduous trees like maples and elms. It represents trees that grow in open areas, exposed to strong winds, which cause their branches to grow straight outwards.
Branches are usually wired to create an orderly appearance. The tree's overall shape should resemble an inverted broom, with the branches evenly spaced on all sides. The trunk should be thicker at the base and taper towards the top. Broom-style bonsai are known for their balanced and formal look.

Cascade Style

The Cascade Style showcases a trunk that descends below the pot's rim, imitating trees growing down a mountain. This style is ideal for conifers and other evergreens. The design evokes the image of a tree growing on a cliff, with its branches and foliage cascading downwards.
Most of the foliage should be located below the pot's rim, and the trunk should gradually taper towards the end. The cascading branches can be wired to achieve the desired shape, creating a sense of movement and grace. Cascade-style bonsai are popular for their dramatic and natural appearance.

Informal Upright Style

The Informal Upright Style mimics the natural growth of trees, with a slightly curved trunk and asymmetrical branching. It works well with various tree species. This style reflects the typical growth patterns of trees that thrive in open spaces with minimal competition.
The trunk should have a gentle curve, resembling the letter 'S,' and the branches should be irregularly spaced to create a more natural look. The tree should give the impression of a mature tree that has withstood the forces of nature. Informal upright-style bonsai are admired for their organic and realistic appearance.

Slanting Style

The Slanting Style features a trunk that slants at an angle, creating a sense of movement and direction. This style is suitable for a wide range of bonsai species. The design represents the natural response of trees that grow on mountain slopes or in windy areas.
The trunk should lean at an angle, and the branches should slant in the same direction as the trunk. The lower branches are usually positioned on the side away from the slant, while the upper branches grow towards the slant. Slanting-style bonsai have a dynamic and visually appealing appearance.

Windswept Style

The Windswept Style portrays the effect of strong winds shaping the tree's growth, with branches all pointing in one direction. This style is best suited for trees that endure strong winds in their natural habitats. It reflects the resilience and determination of trees that adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
The trunk should lean in the direction of the prevailing wind, and the branches should extend in the same direction. The foliage on one side of the tree should be denser, giving the appearance of wind pushing the branches in one direction. Windswept-style bonsai evoke a sense of motion and evoke images of trees facing adversity.

Gear & Tools

Having the right gear and tools is essential for bonsai care. Invest in quality pruning shears, concave cutters, wire cutters, and a root rake. These tools will aid you in maintaining your bonsai's health and appearance.

Pruning

Pruning your bonsai is essential for its health and appearance. Regularly trim back new growth and remove any unwanted branches to maintain the desired shape and size.

Repotting and Soil

Repot your bonsai every 1-2 years to prevent root-bound trees. Use well-draining bonsai soil mixtures to promote healthy root development and refresh the soil's nutrients.

Watering

Proper watering is crucial for the health of your bonsai. Check the soil regularly and water when the top inch feels slightly dry. Avoid overwatering to prevent root rot.

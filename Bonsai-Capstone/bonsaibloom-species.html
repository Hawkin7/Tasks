Species Guide

Japanese Maple (Acer palmatum)

The Japanese Maple, known for its stunning autumn foliage, is a beloved choice for bonsai enthusiasts. It prefers a temperate climate and partial shade to thrive. Pruning and wiring help create its signature delicate branches.
Difficulty level: Moderate.

Read More

Chinese Elm (Ulmus parvifolia)

The Chinese Elm, a versatile species, adapts well to various climates. It features small, serrated leaves and graceful branching. It's relatively low-maintenance and can tolerate beginners' mistakes.
Difficulty level: Easy.

Read More

Juniper (Juniperus)

Junipers are one of the most common species used in bonsai. They come in various forms and sizes, from cascading to upright styles. They are hardy and thrive in full sun. Frequent pruning is essential to maintain their shape.
Difficulty level: Easy.

Read More

Japanese Black Pine (Pinus thunbergii)

The Japanese Black Pine is favored for its rugged appearance and striking black bark. It is well-suited for bonsai and is often used in traditional Japanese gardens. It prefers full sun and well-draining soil.
Difficulty level: Moderate.

Read More

Trident Maple (Acer buergerianum)

The Trident Maple, appreciated for its distinctive three-lobed leaves, is a hardy and adaptable species for bonsai. It prefers a temperate climate and bright, indirect light. Regular pruning and wiring help shape its elegant structure.
Difficulty level: Moderate.

Read More

Ficus (Ficus retusa)

Ficus trees, also known as Banyan figs, are popular indoor bonsai species. They feature attractive aerial roots and glossy foliage. Ficus bonsai prefer warm, tropical conditions and regular pruning to maintain their shape.
Difficulty level: Easy.

Read More

Chinese Juniper (Juniperus chinensis)

The Chinese Juniper is a robust evergreen species that features dense foliage and rugged bark. It prefers full sun and well-draining soil. Wiring and pruning help create artistic shapes. It's ideal for beginners.
Difficulty level: Easy.

Read More

Dwarf Jade (Portulacaria afra)

The Dwarf Jade, also known as the Elephant Bush, is a resilient species well-suited for beginners. It has small, fleshy leaves and can store water in its trunk and branches. It prefers bright light and well-draining soil.
Difficulty level: Easy.

Read More

Japanese White Pine (Pinus parviflora)

The Japanese White Pine is an elegant coniferous species, featuring needles in clusters of five. It prefers a temperate climate with bright light. Regular pinching and pruning are necessary to shape its elegant foliage pads. Difficulty level: Difficult.

Read More

Serissa (Serissa foetida)

The Serissa, also known as the Snow Rose or Tree of a Thousand Stars, is a small flowering bonsai species with tiny, white, star-shaped flowers. It prefers a warm and humid climate. Regular pruning and shaping are essential for its dainty appearance.
Difficulty level: Moderate.
